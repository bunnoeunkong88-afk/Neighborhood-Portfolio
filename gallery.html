Amazing Ancient World of Cambodia

Images Gallery of tourist

Ancient Cambodian temples, particularly those at Angkor, showcase a rich blend of Hindu and Buddhist art and architecture, often featuring intricate bas-relief carvings and towering structures. Angkor Wat, a prominent example, was originally a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later converted to a Buddhist temple. Other important temples include Bayon, known for its giant stone faces, and Ta Prohm, famed for its jungle-covered ruins.

Cambodia Beach
When you’re planning a beach stay in Southeast Asia, you might find yourself invariably drifting toward Thailand. Renowned for its clear blue waters encasing pearly white sand, Thailand’s coastline has grown rapidly in popularity and can be overcrowded. However, right on its doorstep lie Cambodia’s largely undiscovered beaches.
Bokor Mountain
A 32 kilometre road takes you up 1,101 meters up from road 3 near Kampot. At the top is the Bokor Mountain Hill Station, in Bokor National Park.
Kulen Waterfall
Kulen Waterfall, located in Phnom Kulen National Park, is a popular tourist destination in Cambodia. It's known for its scenic beauty and is often included in tours to the park. The waterfall is considered holy water from the Hindu god, Shiva, and is a popular spot for swimming.

Interesting Activities
Play Traditional Games and Dance.
Don’t pass on the opportunity to join the locals who gather at the courtyard of pagodas and other public areas to dance Khmer music and play traditional games.
Visit a Pagoda
For the Khmer New Year, nearly every pagoda of the country is decorated for the occasion. Go to one of them to admire the colorful garlands, flowers.
Visit a Nearby Village
We advise you spend an afternoon in a village nearby Siem Reap if you can. It’s the best way to interact with the local Khmer people and experience an authentic celebration of the Khmer New Year.
